About Ray Lee

Ray Lee is an artist, composer, and performer. His work investigates his fascination with the hidden world of electro-magnetic radiation and in particular how sound can be used as evidence of invisible phenomena. He is interested in the way that science and philosophy represent the universe, and his work questions the orthodoxies that emerge, and submerge, according to the currently fashionable trends. He creates spinning, whirling, and pendulous sound installations and performances that explore “circles of ether,” the invisible forces that surround us.

Chorus

23 & 24 October 2015: University Square, outside Coventry Cathedral

I'm presenting a version of Chorus, my large scale outdoor sound work at INTIME, Coventry on the 23rd and 24th October. Chorus will be located in University Square which is right next to Coventry Cathedral and will be performed at 6pm and 8pm on the 23rd Oct and then from 1pm-3pm as a continuous looped performance on Saturday 24th in the afternoon, with two more performances at 6pm and 7.30pm on Saturday evening. The performances are free and open to the public.

a choir of rotating sirens, their individually strident voices congealing into a thick mutating chord that transfixed listeners in its sticky flux.

David Toop, The Wire
